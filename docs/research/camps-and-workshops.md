# Research: BRC 2026 Recurring Theme Camps & Workshops

The official 2026 Camps page lists *applications*, not confirmed placements, but appearance is a strong signal. Confirmed applying for 2026: **Ashram Galactica, Barbie Death Village, BAAAHS, Gigsville**.

## ⚠️ Honesty flags — NOT recurring in 2026 (excluded from the deck)
- **Death Guild Thunderdome** — sitting out Burning Man 2026 (still runs Wasteland Weekend).
- **Comfort & Joy** (queer camp) — taking 2026 off; **7 Sins Lounge** closed after 2025.
- **Center Camp** persists as civic heart, but the Café **stopped selling coffee in 2022** (decommodification) — it's now a plaza/performance canopy.
- **Human Carcass Wash** is host-dependent (PolyParadise) — verify host each year (rated MED).

## Card seeds (14 near-certain recurring)
```
1. Center Camp — there's always a center to return to; walk toward the heart when lost. Trunk. Dare: go at your most disoriented hour, take a couch, let a stranger tell their whole story first. [high]
2. Playa Info & the Rangers — asking for help is a skill; guides carry care, not authority. Trunk. Dare: ask "what should I do today?" then do it; or turn in something to Lost & Found. [high]
3. Costco Soulmate Trading Outlet — you can't shop your way to love; show up honest, be matched. Branches. Dare: fill the intake form honestly, accept the match, go find the stranger. [high]
4. Ashram Galactica — luxury is a gift you give; receive strangers like royalty. Branches. Dare: arrive filthy, be fawned over, enter the yurt raffle; return as staff and serve champagne. [high]
5. Barbie Death Village (Dream Chipper) — feed the manufactured, perfected self to the chipper. Shell. Dare: sacrifice a Barbie, name aloud the manufactured self you're killing. [high]
6. The Human Carcass Wash — radical trust + stated boundaries; let strangers wash the week off you, then wash the next person. Roots. Dare: state boundaries out loud, get washed, join the line. [med (host-dependent)]
7. Black Rock Bakery — the best gifts have a price that isn't money; nourishment for honesty. Shell. Dare: trade a true, hard-to-say story for a warm pastry. [high]
8. The Zendo Project — there are no bad trips, only difficult ones; you don't go through it alone. Roots. Dare: walk a struggling stranger there and stay; or volunteer a shift. [high]
9. The Orgy Dome (Down Low Club) — desire is sacred when consensual; freedom and boundaries are one structure. Roots/Trunk. Dare: ask out loud for exactly what you want; receive a graceful "no." [high]
10. Consent Culture (B.E.D.) — yes means yes, no means no; consent is rehearsed, not assumed. Trunk. Dare: take a consent workshop; practice an embodied "no" and "yes." [high archetype]
11. The Tea House — slow down; stillness is its own ceremony. Branches/Trunk. Dare: sit a full tea ceremony, no phone, no agenda, till you lose track of time. [high archetype]
12. Ecstatic Dance at Dawn — dance your prayer at the edge of night. Branches. Dare: bike out in the dark to a sunrise set, dance wordless till the sun clears the mountains. [high archetype]
13. Sound Bath & Sunrise Yoga — the desert cracks you open; tend the body or it tends you the hard way. Roots. Dare: lie down for a full sound bath at peak heat, let the bowls empty your head. [high archetype]
14. Gigsville — take the work seriously, never yourself; the elders of chaos keep the trickster alive. Shell. Dare: accept whatever absurd challenge they hand you. [high]
```
